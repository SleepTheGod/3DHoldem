3D Holdem

3D Holdem is a type of Poker with great depth of strategy and resistance to computer analysis.

Rules

As in Texas Holdem all players get two hole cards of their own which only they can see and there are four rounds of betting with community cards dealt out between them. Community cards are dealt out in three rows, which first get three cards each on the flop, then one more each on the turn, and finally one last card to each row on the river. In the end there are fifteen community cards on the table, three rows with five each. A player's hand is the best 5-card poker hand which can be made using their two hole cards plus one community card from each row.

Unlike in other poker variants, in 3D Holdem a flush beats a full house (see statistics below for why). All other hand rankings are the same.

Example

In this example, your hole cards are:

J♡ 4♡

You can potentially make a flush but not a straight because both of your hole cards must be in your final hand.

The flop comes down like this:

A♢ 5♣ J♣
2♡ A♠ K♢
7♡ Q♡ A♣

Now your best hand is A♢ from the first row, A♠ from the second row, and A♣ from the third row giving you three aces with a jack and four kicker. You don't make a full house (aces over jacks) because your hand must include your 4♡. You also don't make a flush because even though there are three hearts on the board two of them are on the same row and you have to take exactly one card from each row. You're behind anything including the other ace, which makes four aces, any pocket pair, which makes a full house, and any queen or king, which makes three aces with a higher kicker.

Here is the turn:

A♢ 5♣ J♣ 8♡
2♡ A♠ K♢ 8♣
7♡ Q♡ A♣ J♢

Your hand has improved to a flush using the 8♡, 2♡ and Q♡. Since in 3D Holdem flushes beat full houses you now beat a pocket pair.

Finally the river:

A♢ 5♣ J♣ 8♡ A♡
2♡ A♠ K♢ 8♣ J♠
7♡ Q♡ A♣ J♢ K♣

Now you've made four jacks and because all four aces are on the board you know that nobody can beat you with four aces. But pocket kings will beat you with four kings.

Advantages

3D Holdem has fast play and simple rules like its close relatives Texas Holdem and Omaha. It has a much wider variety of situations which can arise because more cards are dealt out and some of them can act as blockers, which gives it greater strategic depth. Players being stuck with both of their hole cards creates more potential for reads. The larger number of hands also makes it much more difficult for computers to analyze.

Origins

3D Holdem was inspired by 5 Card Omaha and first posted about here. The property that flush should beat full house was discovered while putting this page together.

In 3D Holdem flush beats full house because it has lower probability. This happens because of the pigeonhole principle, which applies more to full house than flush, but the details are complex and the chances were fairly close to begin with.

In the Omahas it looks like three of a kind is less likely than a straight but that's misleading. A full house is upgraded but still a three of a kind. Vanilla three of a kind plus full house is greater than straight. In Texas Hold'em pair beats high card for the same reason.

Also in the Omahas two pair is particularly likely because there's often a pair on the board and one in the hole cards because the birthday paradox applies to both. Pairs act sort of like higher high cards.
